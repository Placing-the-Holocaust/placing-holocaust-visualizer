QUESTION: This is Susan Bachrach. I am at the home of Mr. Emanuel Munzer. I am representing the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum and Mr. Munzer is going to tell us about his life before and during the war. Could you tell us a little bit about your family background, where you were born and what your father did, your school background? Those sorts of things?

ANSWER: I was born in Berlin on February 26, 1920, in Berlin , Germany -- as the only son of my parents, what should I say?

QUESTION: Give your parents’ names.

ANSWER: Hans and Margaret. I had pretty normal schooling. Went through kindergarten , pre-school and --.

QUESTION: Did you go to public school ?

ANSWER: Public school and after that I went to gymnasium with the intention to go to university and become an electrical engineer and go through to my Ph.D. This was interrupted by the advent of the Nazis in 1933.

QUESTION: What was your father doing? Did you always live in Berlin with your parents?

ANSWER: I always lived in Berlin , yes, with my parents.

QUESTION: What sort of neighborhood did you live in? Do you recall?

ANSWER: It was a well-to-do neighborhood. I was in the southeastern district , south western district of Berlin . It was a district called Schoeneberg . I had a good childhood.

QUESTION: Was it a Jewish neighborhood ?

ANSWER: No, no, was mixed. There were many Jews living there and many non- Jews.

ANSWER: We lived in an apartment building . We lived on the fourth floor . My father always complained about being bald, that when he carried me these four flights up the stores , I amused myself pulling out his hair.

QUESTION: So you spent all your childhood in this apartment ?

ANSWER: That’s right.
